What is a Heatwave?

A heatwave is a prolonged period of abnormally high temperatures. In India, heatwaves are declared by the IMD when the maximum temperature exceeds 40째C in the plains and 30째C in hilly regions. These events are more frequent between March and June. They can cause serious health problems including dehydration, heat exhaustion, and heatstroke.

Symptoms of Heatstroke

  • Body temperature above 103째F (39.4째C)
  • Hot, red, dry, or damp skin
  • Dizziness or fainting
  • Confusion or slurred speech
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Rapid breathing and heartbeat
  • Loss of consciousness (coma)

If any of these symptoms occur, call emergency services and move the person to a cooler place immediately.

Official Heatwave Safety Tips

  1. Drink water frequently, even if not thirsty. Avoid caffeinated or sugary drinks.
  2. Stay indoors between 12 PM to 4 PM. Reschedule strenuous outdoor activities.
  3. Wear lightweight, light-colored, loose-fitting cotton clothes and use a wide-brim hat or umbrella outdoors.
  4. Take cool showers or sponge baths during the day. Use fans or air coolers to maintain indoor ventilation.
  5. Keep windows and curtains closed during the hottest part of the day. Open them at night for ventilation.
  6. Check on elderly persons, children, and pets regularly.
  7. Never leave people or animals inside parked vehicles.
  8. Eat light, fresh meals. Avoid oily or high-protein food during heatwave days.

Tips verified by NDMA & WHO guidelines.
